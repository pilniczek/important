## Page types

Each page is tagged with one of the following types, shown as a colored pill in the table above.

- **Tool** — A page whose subject is a named 3rd-party library, framework, SaaS, font, or asset. Content can be a core-principles overview. Can be a collection of tools, a visual-only reference, or a cross-functional combination of two distinct tools. Unique per subject.
- **Tutorial / How To** — A page that walks through accomplishing a specific task. Action-oriented and reproducible; the spine is procedural/instructional.
- **Approach** — A page capturing a methodology, philosophy, framework, or set of principles — the "why" or "how to think about" something. Includes team/meeting practices and decision frameworks. Unique per subject.
- **Code / Snippet** — A page whose spine is reusable code/config, or a collection of related code recipes/commands clustered around one topic.
- **Course** — A page that primarily points to 3rd-party learning material. Qualifies via a `url:` frontmatter field, multiple external URLs in body, or embedded video/PDF.
- **Article** — User-authored content that was published on a 3rd-party blog.
